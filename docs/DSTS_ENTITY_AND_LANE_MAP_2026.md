# 1. PURPOSE

This file defines:
- which entity is used for which lane
- which kinds of money belong in which lane
- what each entity may or may not say publicly
- how to avoid lane confusion across DSTS

The goal is not to remove legal limits.  
The goal is to map every program into the correct lane.

# 3. LEGAL LANE MAP

## Lane 1 — Commercial
### Money types
- program fee
- script journey fee
- membership fee
- event ticket or program fee
- creator product payment
- commercial sponsor package

### Entities
- Viet Can Star Entertainment JSC
- Viet Can New Corp

### Public language
- commercial program
- official operator
- paid participation
- terms and refund apply

---

## Lane 2 — Support / Sponsor / Philanthropy
### Money types
- support interest
- sponsor interest
- scholarship support
- dream kit support
- grant-like support

### Entity
- Angel Edu Tam Foundation when legally ready
- until then: inquiry-first / no public checkout

### Public language
- support interest
- sponsor interest
- partner support
- public-benefit support inquiry

### Avoid
- donate now
- tax-deductible unless actually verified and lawful
- support with return
- sponsor as investment

---

## Lane 3 — Media & IP
### Money types
- licensing
- content rights
- media package
- showcase distribution
- creator IP agreements

### Entity
- commercial lane entities, structured with separate documentation

### Public language
- scripts
- stories
- media
- showcase
- cultural production

---

## Lane 4 — Community Infrastructure
### Money types
Generally not public-first money lane.
This lane is for:
- identity
- verification
- archive
- community routing
- diaspora memory
- private systems

### Public language
- community
- belonging
- identity
- trust
- archive
- groups

---

## Lane 5 — Investment
### Money types
- strategic investor interest
- future founder fund
- future talent investment
- future legal investment opportunities

### Entity
Separate structure later. Not public now.

### Public language
None in ordinary public flows.

### Rule
Always gated. Never mixed with child support or sponsor language.

## Future investor or strategic capital discussions
Lane: Investment, gated only

# 5. PAYMENT RULES

## Rule 1
Do not use one payment lane for all programs.

## Rule 2
Do not use founder personal payment identities for public commercial operations.

## Rule 3
Do not publicly present commercial entities as if they are charitable entities.

## Rule 4
Do not publicly accept child-support style payments through a commercial page if the legal/support lane is not ready.

## Rule 5
Do not let support, sponsorship, purchase, and investment appear to be the same action.

# 7. REQUIRED INTERNAL CONTROLS

Every DSTS program must be marked with:
- lane
- operator entity
- payment route
- refund rule
- support/disclaimer rule
- legal reviewer
- public claim restrictions

This must exist before launch.

---

# 8. MINIMUM INTERNAL REGISTRY FIELDS

For each program:
- program_name
- public_name
- internal_code
- lane_type
- commercial_or_support
- child_related (yes/no)
- operator_entity
- payment_allowed (yes/no)
- public_payment_allowed (yes/no)
- support_interest_allowed (yes/no)
- investment_related (yes/no)
- legal_reviewer
- child_safety_reviewer
- public_status

---

# 9. FINAL DIRECTIVE

No DSTS team member may:
- invent a payment route
- choose an entity informally
- call support “investment”
- call sponsorship “donation” unless legally correct
- mix child-facing language with commercial sales
- publish public financial promises

Every program must know its lane before it launches.

---

# 10. FINAL CONCLUSION

DSTS becomes scalable not by removing legal structure, but by using the correct structure.

The safest and strongest model is:
- commercial entity for commercial programs
- support/nonprofit lane for social-benefit support
- media/IP lane for stories and content
- community lane for identity and trust
- investment lane only later, gated
