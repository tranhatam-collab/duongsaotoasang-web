### 1.2 Option A — Vietnam Only: Quỹ Xã Hội / Quỹ Từ Thiện per Nghị Định 03/2026/NĐ-CP

**Legal basis:** Nghị định 03/2026/NĐ-CP (ban hành 09/01/2026, hiệu lực **01/03/2026**), replacing NĐ 93/2019 and NĐ 136/2024.

**Entity type:** Quỹ xã hội (social fund) or Quỹ từ thiện (charitable fund) with full legal personality (tư cách pháp nhân).

**Requirements under NĐ 03/2026:**

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Physical office in VN | Required — address with legal use documentation |
| Legal personality | Full tư cách pháp nhân, con dấu, tài khoản riêng |
| Non-distribution constraint | Assets may not be distributed to founders/members |
| Foreign founder participation | Permitted — overseas Vietnamese may co-found |
| Public financial reporting | Báo cáo công khai "đầu vào" + "đầu ra" before March 31 each year |
| Quarterly financial reports | Required |
| Annual audit | Recommended; required if >20 billion VND |
| No deposit-taking or lending | Quỹ cannot accept deposits or issue loans |
| Foreign contracts | Copies submitted to supervising authority within 7 days |
| Supervising ministry | Bộ Nội vụ (Ministry of Home Affairs) |

**Application process (estimated):**
- Prepare hồ sơ: điều lệ + tài sản ban đầu (minimum per NĐ 03/2026: see Legal Counsel for current threshold) + danh sách sáng lập viên
- Submit to Bộ Nội vụ or provincial People's Committee (depending on scope)
- Review period: 45-60 working days
- Additional time for corrections: variable
- **Total estimated timeline: 3-6 months**
- **Estimated cost: Legal Counsel $3-5K (retainer) + official fees ~10-20M VND**

**Tax treatment in VN:**
- Exempt from CIT on qualifying charitable income
- GTGT (VAT): charitable activities generally exempt; commercial services side (if any) subject to 10%
- PIT withholding: applies to staff compensation normally

**Advantages (Option A):**
- Directly captures VN diaspora donations in VND
- Easier to run VN-based programs (hire VN staff, rent VN venues)
- Recognized by VN government for child welfare partnerships

**Disadvantages (Option A):**
- Donations from US/AU/UK/EU donors NOT US-tax-deductible
- International grant funders (Gates Foundation, Ford Foundation) typically require 501(c)(3) or equivalent
- Cannot issue US Form 1099 / tax receipts for diaspora donors

**⚠️ FOUNDER DECISION #1 — if Option A selected:** Confirm city/province for registration, initial asset amount, founding board member names.

### 1.4 Option C — Dual Entity (RECOMMENDED)

**Structure:**
- **US:** Angel Edu Tam Foundation Inc (or new 501(c)(3)) handles US/AU/CA/EU donations
- **VN:** Quỹ xã hội under NĐ 03/2026 handles VN operations, VN donations, VN grants

**Legal firewall between entities:**
- Separate bank accounts, separate books, separate boards
- No loans or cross-subsidization between entities
- Coordination via formal program agreement (contract reviewed by both legal counsels)
- US entity grants funds to VN entity via formal grant agreement (common in international NGO structure)

**Key requirement:** Intercompany grant agreement must comply with:
- IRS rules on expenditure responsibility (IRC §4945) for grants to foreign organizations
- VN rules on receiving foreign funds (NĐ 80/2020/NĐ-CP — quy định về tiếp nhận viện trợ nước ngoài)
- Currency regulations: State Bank of VN approval may be required for transfers >$50K

**Advantages (Option C):**
- Maximizes donor base (US-deductible + VN-deductible separately)
- Enables VN government partnerships (Bộ Nội vụ recognition)
- Diaspora model strength: donors globally can give to their preferred entity
- Risk segmentation: legal issue in one entity does not automatically affect other

**Disadvantages (Option C):**
- Higher legal and accounting cost (two sets of filings, two audits)
- More complex governance (two boards, two sets of bylaws)
- Grant transfer between entities requires paperwork each time

**Recommended by this document:** Option C. The diaspora fundraising model and VN program operations strongly benefit from both entities. However, Option C can be phased:
- Y1: Start with whichever entity is faster (US if Angel Edu Tam Foundation Inc is active; VN if legal filing is faster)
- Y2: Add second entity
- Y3: Full dual-entity operation

## Mục 2 — Fiscal Sponsor Option (Y1 Bridge)

### 2.1 What is Fiscal Sponsorship?

A fiscal sponsor is an existing 501(c)(3) organization that provides its tax-exempt status and financial infrastructure to a project that doesn't yet have its own tax-exempt status. Donors donate to the fiscal sponsor, which passes funds to the project.

**Result:** NDNUM can begin raising US tax-deductible donations in **2-4 weeks** instead of waiting 6-12 months for IRS 501(c)(3) approval.

### 2.2 Recommended Fiscal Sponsors

| Organization | Model | Fee | Setup Time | Notes |
|-------------|-------|-----|------------|-------|
| **Open Collective** | Model A (direct project) | 5-8% of donations | 1-2 weeks | Online-first, global, transparent public ledger |
| **Tides Foundation** | Model A or C | 7-9% + $2-5K setup | 2-4 weeks | Prestigious, many diaspora and arts nonprofits use it |
| **Community Initiatives** | Model A | 9% | 2-4 weeks | SF-based, arts and social justice focus |
| **Friends of the Children** | — | N/A | Not applicable | FOTC is a program model, not a fiscal sponsor |

**Model A (Direct Project):** The fiscal sponsor is the legal employer and holds all funds. The project director is typically an employee or contractor of the fiscal sponsor.

**Model C (Pre-approved Grant):** The project maintains its own legal identity; the fiscal sponsor reviews and approves grants. More independence but more overhead.

**NDNUM recommendation:** Open Collective (Model A) for Y1 due to speed, transparency (public ledger builds donor trust), and low overhead. Switch to own 501(c)(3) or keep fiscal sponsor based on donation volume by Y2.

### 2.3 Fiscal Sponsor Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Fiscal sponsor holds fiduciary control | Sign comprehensive project agreement with exit clause |
| Sponsor organization folds or changes mission | Choose established org (10+ year track record) |
| 9-10% fee reduces program dollars | Transparently disclose fee to donors; offset by faster start |
| Donor confusion about which entity they're giving to | Clear communication: "NDNUM is a project of [Fiscal Sponsor]" |
| Cannot hire independent staff | Coordinator hired as fiscal sponsor contractors initially |

### 2.4 Fiscal Sponsor Timeline

```
Week 1-2: Research + select fiscal sponsor, apply
Week 3-4: Fiscal sponsor review + approval
Week 4: Open Collective page live OR Tides project page live
Week 5+: Begin US fundraising with tax-deductible receipts
```

**Parallel track:** While fiscal sponsor is active, file Form 1023 for Angel Edu Tam Foundation Inc (or new 501(c)(3)). Once IRS approves, transition fiscal sponsor relationship to own entity.

```
                          │
                   ZERO CROSS-TRANSFER
                (any transfer requires outside legal opinion)
                          │
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   LANE B — NONPROFIT                         │
│  Revenue: Donations, grants, sponsor fees, membership B      │
│  Entity: Angel Edu Tam Foundation Inc / VN Quỹ / Fiscal Spon │
│  Bank: Lane B nonprofit checking (separate from Lane A)      │
│  Tax: Exempt (qualifying income); Form 990; NĐ 03/2026 report│

└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                          │
                   ZERO CROSS-TRANSFER
                          │
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   LANE C — INVESTMENT                        │
│  Revenue: Investor commitments (Quỹ Bệ Phóng)               │
│  Entity: Separate investment vehicle (not yet established)   │
│  Status: ⚠️ CLOSED Y1 — see Decision FD-4 below            │
│  Requirement: Securities law compliance + KYC + investor qual│
│  Cannot open before: Legal Counsel (securities) signed off   │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 3.2 Lane Firewall Rules (Non-Negotiable)

| Rule | Violation consequence |
|------|----------------------|
| No transfer from Lane A (commercial) to Lane B (nonprofit) | Tax fraud in multiple jurisdictions; donor deception |
| No transfer from Lane B (nonprofit) to Lane A (commercial) | IRS private benefit doctrine violation; 501(c)(3) revocation risk |
| No transfer from Lane C (investment) to Lane B (nonprofit) | Securities law violation (investor funds cannot be donated away) |
| Every donation receipt must specify Lane B entity by name | Compliance with IRS acknowledgement requirements |
| No "we'll sort out which account later" accounting | Immediate reclassification is not possible once deposited |
| Lane B funds may only be spent on program activities | Mission test: every expense must advance the charitable purpose |

**What IS allowed:**
- Lane A paying market-rate fees to Lane B for services (e.g., Lane B produces a youth arts event; Lane A event ticket sales fund the space rental; Lane B charges Lane A fair market rent for use of nonprofit space) — requires invoice + arm's length pricing
- Lane A Founder/staff time volunteering for Lane B NDNUM programs (zero charge, documented)
- Public communications that reference all 3 lanes as part of DSTS ecosystem (as long as Lane B is clearly identified as separate legal entity)

### 3.3 Lane C — Investment Lane Status

**Current status:** CLOSED Y1 (2026-2027).

The Investment Lane (Quỹ Bệ Phóng) is the vehicle for qualified investor participation in DSTS/NDNUM growth. It requires:
- Securities law compliance (Luật Chứng khoán VN 2019 / SEC regulations if US investors)
- Investor qualification framework (accredited investor standard)
- Private placement memorandum (PPM) or equivalent
- KYC/AML for all investors
- Dedicated legal counsel for securities law
- Separate investment entity (not the nonprofit, not the commercial company)

**Why closed Y1:**
- Legal complexity and cost ($20-50K to set up properly)
- Founder bandwidth: Y1 is for program delivery, not investor relations
- Reputational risk: mixing investment solicitation with charitable program narrative confuses donors and investors both
- Regulatory risk: premature investment solicitation without proper filings is a securities law violation

**Conditions to open Lane C (any of these trigger review):**
- DSTS reaches $1M ARR (indicates scale that justifies investment infrastructure)
- Founder hires dedicated CFO + securities counsel
- 501(c)(3) or VN Quỹ fully established and audited for at least 1 year
- Investor has approached with term sheet (reactive, not proactive)

**⚠️ FOUNDER DECISION #3 (FD-4):** Confirm Lane C remains closed Y1 (2026) and Y2 (2027), with review at end of Y2. If Founder wants to open Lane C sooner, outside securities counsel must be engaged first.

### 6.3 Anti-Money Laundering / KYC for Donors and Sponsors

KYC requirements based on donation/sponsor amount:

| Amount | VN | US |
|--------|----|----|
| < $2,500 (< 50M VND) | Name + email only | Name + email only |
| $2,500 – $10,000 | CCCD/passport copy | Government ID copy |
| $10,001 – $50,000 | CCCD + source of funds declaration | Government ID + beneficial ownership declaration |
| > $50,000 | Full AML clearance (see below) + outside counsel review | SAR consideration threshold; Enhanced Due Diligence |

**Full AML clearance for > $50,000:**
1. Source of funds: written declaration + supporting documentation (bank statements, business registration, etc.)
2. Beneficial ownership: who ultimately benefits from this donation (if corporate donor)
3. Screening: check against OFAC SDN list (US), VN Ministry of Public Security sanctions list
4. PEP (Politically Exposed Person) check: if donor is government official, enhanced scrutiny
5. Legal Counsel review before accepting

**Automatic rejection triggers (no legal override):**
- Tobacco companies
- Gambling companies (casinos, online gambling, lottery operators)
- Predatory financial services (payday lending, rent-to-own with APR > 100%)
- Companies with record of anti-LGBTQ+ discrimination
- Companies on OFAC SDN list
- Anonymous donations > $10,000 (cannot verify KYC)

## Mục 7 — Board Governance

### 7.1 Minimum Board Requirements

**US 501(c)(3):**
- Minimum 3 directors (most states)
- Majority must be independent (not employees, not family of employees)
- Annual board meeting required
- Meeting minutes retained permanently
- Conflict of interest policy required (IRS Form 990 Schedule O asks about it)

**VN Quỹ per NĐ 03/2026:**
- Hội đồng quản lý (Board): minimum 5 thành viên (check NĐ 03/2026 for exact requirement)
- Ban kiểm soát (Supervisory Board): required for quỹ above certain size
- Ban điều hành (Management Board): separate from Board

**CSO independence requirement (from NDNUM_CHILD_SAFETY_POLICY.md):**
- Child Safety Officer reports to Board directly, not to Founder or operations
- CSO cannot be unilaterally terminated by Founder — requires Board vote
- This must be written into articles of incorporation / điều lệ

### 7.2 Founder Conflicts of Interest Policy

Founder (Trần Hà Tâm) must annually disclose:
- Any paid relationship with any entity that does business with NDNUM
- Any personal benefit (direct or indirect) from NDNUM programs or funds
- Any family member employed by or under contract with NDNUM

**IRS rules on self-dealing (501(c)(3)):**
- Founder cannot receive below-market loans from 501(c)(3)
- Founder compensation (if any) must be set by independent board process based on comparability data
- Founder's family members cannot be employed at above-market rates

**NDNUM Year 1 policy:** Founder receives no salary from Lane B (nonprofit). Founder may receive market-rate compensation from Lane A (commercial) for commercial activities.
