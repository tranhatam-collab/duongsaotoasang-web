# 1. PURPOSE

This document defines exactly what must be rewritten on the public-facing DSTS site so that:

- the site stops mixing legal lanes
- the site becomes clearer and safer
- the site can support long-term program expansion
- NDNUM can exist publicly in roadmap mode without creating child-safety or legal risk
- commercial, support, and future investment logic no longer collide in the same surface

This is a **rewrite scope**, not a full design system or code spec.

---

# 2. CURRENT PROBLEM TO FIX

The public site currently risks mixing 3 narratives too closely:

1. **Content / culture / community platform**
2. **High-ticket commercial offer**
3. **Fundraising / sponsor / social-impact language**

This creates confusion for users, partners, payment providers, and legal reviewers.

### Main risk outcomes
- users do not know whether they are buying, supporting, or investing
- sponsor language may be misread as donation or investment
- child/youth program pages may appear too close to commercial or fame/success language
- trust is reduced because the public surface feels mixed

---

# 3. TARGET PUBLIC POSITIONING

The public site must present DSTS as:

**A global creative, journey, and community platform for Vietnamese people worldwide.**

It must not present DSTS as:
- a charity fund
- an investment platform
- a get-famous engine
- a get-rich promise engine
- a child intake platform
- a coaching marketplace

---

# 5. REWRITE OBJECTIVES

The rewrite must achieve 8 outcomes:

1. A new visitor understands what DSTS is within seconds
2. The public site clearly separates commercial offerings from support/sponsor pathways
3. High-ticket programs have their own commercial boundary and do not contaminate the entire homepage
4. NDNUM appears only as a safe, preparing, roadmap-first program
5. The site no longer uses vague fundraising-like language near commercial offers
6. Sponsorship is framed as **support interest / sponsor interest**, not as investment
7. The public site does not overclaim outcomes like fame, income, or guaranteed success
8. The site becomes legally safer to expand later

# 7. HOMEPAGE REWRITE SCOPE

## 7.1 Homepage must keep
- identity of DSTS
- cultural / creative / journey narrative
- community direction
- core programs overview
- scripts / showcase direction
- events
- posts / stories / transparency
- support / sponsor interest CTA

## 7.2 Homepage must stop doing
- mixing all money lanes together
- presenting high-ticket offers too close to support/child lanes
- implying a public fundraising platform
- implying donation language if the legal lane is not ready
- implying celebrity / success / fame outcomes

## 7.3 Homepage structural sections

### Section 1 — Hero
Clear description of what DSTS is.

### Section 2 — What DSTS does
Creative journeys, programs, community, Vietnamese global connection.

### Section 3 — Core program families
- journeys
- scripts
- showcases
- community experiences
- future youth and social programs (carefully framed)

### Section 4 — Stories and real people
Human-centered proof.

### Section 5 — Events / programs / pathways
Structured participation.

### Section 6 — Transparency
How DSTS is built, what is public, what is preparing.

### Section 7 — Support / sponsor / partner interest
Non-checkout, inquiry-first.

### Section 8 — Footer / legal boundary
Commercial, support, and future lanes clearly separated.

# 8. HIGH-TICKET COMMERCIAL OFFER RULES

## Any high-ticket pages must:
- live on their own route
- have clear commercial terms
- identify the operator
- avoid being framed as social support
- avoid adjacent child-program framing
- avoid “this changes your life” outcome guarantees
- include refund / eligibility / no-outcome-guarantee language

## Example route types
- `/programs/...`
- `/scripts/...`
- `/journeys/...`

## Example must-have sections
- who it is for
- what it includes
- what it does not guarantee
- eligibility
- operator
- terms / refund / contact

---

# 9. NDNUM PUBLIC REWRITE RULES

## NDNUM on public site must be:
- roadmap only
- preparing only
- child-safe wording only
- no intake, no payment, no mentor matching

## NDNUM public allowed elements
- vision
- why it exists
- who it hopes to serve later
- child safety commitment
- guardian-first statement
- waitlist / interest / partner inquiry

## NDNUM public blocked elements
- child registration
- child account creation
- public child profiles
- public child stories without explicit review and lawful basis
- mentor-child matching
- sponsor payment checkout
- any success/fame/income promise

---

# 10. TRANSPARENCY PAGE REQUIREMENTS

The transparency page should become the public trust buffer.

It should explain:
- what DSTS is
- what DSTS is not
- what lanes exist
- which things are commercial
- which things are only support interest
- which things are not public yet
- why NDNUM is in preparation mode

It must not become:
- a legal wall of text only
- a fundraising brochure
- a vague emotional page with no operational clarity

---

# 11. SUPPORT / SPONSOR INTEREST PAGE REQUIREMENTS

The site should have a clear public-safe page for:
- sponsor interest
- support interest
- volunteer interest
- partner interest

## This page must:
- be inquiry-first
- not take public child-related payments yet
- not frame sponsor as investor
- not frame support as profit-linked
- not promise control over beneficiaries or recipients

# 15. DEFINITION OF DONE

This rewrite scope is done only if:
- the homepage no longer mixes commercial, support, and child program lanes in one confusing narrative
- NDNUM is public only as roadmap/preparing mode
- support/sponsor inquiry is clear and non-investment-framed
- commercial programs have their own boundary
- public trust and clarity improve
- the site can scale later without rewriting the entire legal story again

---

# 16. FINAL DIRECTIVE

Do not rebuild DSTS as a generic marketing site.

Rebuild it as:
**a clear public-facing global creative and journey platform with separated lanes and safer expansion logic**
